vada

Medu Vada is a spicy and savoury doughnut shape like fritter made with urad dal, onion, spices, and seasoning.In South India and Sri Lanka, medu vada, along with idli, dosa, sambar, and chutney, is the most popular vegetarian breakfast dish.

prep time

35 min.

cook time

20 min.

serving

2 servings

instructions

step 1

It is essential to soak the dal for vada for at least 5 – 6 hours before grinding. The vada batter is never prepared with unsoaked lentils.Use very little water while grinding the dal (by little I mean not more than 2 – 4 tablespoons). If the batter seems thick, add a tablespoon of water at a time. It is a time and labour-intensive process but worth it.

step 2

After grinding without adding salt, whisk the batter for 10 minutes or till it is light, airy, and fluffy. I use my electric hand mixer for this purpose.It does the job excellently. The perfectly fluffy dal batter results in a delicious restaurant-style vada.

step 3

It requires a bit of practice. Always wet your fingers with water before shaping the vada, or you can use a banana leaf also for the purpose.Before deep-frying the vada, ensure the oil is hot enough. Perform a test by dropping a small drop of batter into the oil. If it quickly floats to the surface, oil is ready. But if it sinks or gets stuck to the pan, wait for more time.

step 4

I prefer frying the vada at medium-high heat so that it gets perfectly cooked from the inside and crisp from the outside.Let the vada cook and turn golden from one side, then flip, and cook from the other side.For an authentic South Indian breakfast at home, prepare – Rava Idli, Medu Vada, Sambar, Coconut Chutney, and filter coffee. It will be a blissful Sunday morning breakfast.

ingredients

½ cup urad dal

2 tablespoon chana dal

½ teaspoon methi seeds

1½ cups raw rice

tools

Hand Blender

Large Heavy Pot With Lid

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups
